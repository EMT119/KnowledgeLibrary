Anatomy and Physiology Review: The Female Reproductive System

The female reproductive system is made up of the ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, cervix, vagina, and breasts. Hormones will stimulate the ovaries to produce oocytes which causes it to undergo oogenesis that will result in a mature egg (ovum). The ovum is released approximately every 28 days. The egg travels through the fallopian tubes, these tubes extend from the uterus to the ovary where fertilization normally occurs. The fallopian tubes then exit into the uterus.

During a menstrual cycel the lining of the uterus thickens in preparation for pregnancy. If fertilization occurs then the egg will embed itself in the lining of the uterus, however if fertilization does not occur then the uterus will shed which causes what we know as menstruation

The vagina is what receives the male penis during sexual intercour. The muscular walls of the vagina are able to expand and stretcher to allow childbirth. Female genitalia include the vulva, labia minor, the clitoris, prepuce, labia major, and mons pubis. The periuneum is the area between the urethral opening and the anus. It includes skin, external genitalia, and underlysing tissues.

Gestation

Gestation refers to the process of feta development following fertilization of an egg. A fertilized egg is called a zygote, when this egg is fertilized (usually in the distal third of the fallopian tube) it will move from the fallopian tube and to the uterus. The zygote will being progressive cell divison. After the embryo contains approximately 32 cells it will implant in the uterine wall. As mentioned above the uterine wall has thickened in preparation for implantation, this thickening is caused by progeterone. Implanation generally takes place 7 days after fertilization. There are two groups that split to become the embryo and placenta. The embryo is formed from the inner group of blastocyst cells known as embyroblast. The placenta is formed from the outer group of cells known as the trophoblast. During fertilization to the end of nine weeks the blastocyst is referred to as a an embyro, however 10 weeks and beyond it is known as a fetus
