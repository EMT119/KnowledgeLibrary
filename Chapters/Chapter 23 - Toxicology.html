Overview of Substance Abuse

  • Drug Abuse
    • Any use of drugs that causes physical, psychological, economic, legal, or social harm to the user or to others
  • Habituation
    • Psychological dependence on a drug or drugs
  • Physical Dependence
    • Physiologic state of adapation to a drug. Can cause withdrawal syndrome if drug is stopped
  • Phsychological Dependence
    • Emotional state of craving a drug to maintain a feeling of well-being
  • Tolerance
    • Physiologic adaptation to the effects of a drug, this means larger doses are needed to achieve the same effect
  • Withdrawal Syndrome
    • Predicatble set of signs and symptoms that occur after abrupt cessation of a drug
  • Drug Addiction
    • Compulsive use of a substance resulting in physical psychological or social harm to the user, who continues the use of the substance depsite the harm
  • Antagonist
    • Something that counteracts the actions of something else. In relation to drugs it prevents the drugs ability to bind to a cell receptor
  • Potentiation
    • Enhancement of the effect of one drug by another drug
  • Synergism
    • Action of two substances in which the toal effect is greater than the sum of the independent effects of the two substances

It is important drug abuse can effect any age and social status. However, adolescents are susceptible to experimentation with drugs

Pathophysiology, Assessment, and Management of Specific Poisons

Alcohol

Alcohol is a power CNS depressant. It is both a sedative and hypnotic.

Withdrawal Symptoms

Minor

  • Restlessness
  • Anxiousness
  • Sleeping problems
  • Agitation
  • Tremors

Major

  • Increased blood pressure
  • Vomiting
  • Hallucinations

Patients can experience delirium tremens (DTs) otherwise known as alcohol withdrawal delirium. This can result in tremors, restlessness, weakness, fever, diaphoresis, disorientation, hallucinations, confusion, hypotension, seizures, and possibly death
