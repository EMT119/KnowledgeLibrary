Anatomy and Physiology Review

The nervous system is divided into two parts: central and peripheral. The central nervous system (CNS) is composed of the brain and spinal cord. The peripheral nervous system conducts sensory and motor impulses to and from the skin, muscles, and other organs to the spinal cord.

The Skull

The skull is broken into two group: cranium and facial bones. The cranium is several thick bones fused together and it functions to hold and protect the brain. It is comprised of 80% brain tissue, 10% blood supply, and 10% cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)

The bone on the back of the skull is known as the occipital bone, the bones on the lateral side of the skull are called the temporal regions and between them lie the parietal bones. The forehead is composed of the frontal bone.

The face is composed of 14 bones. The upper, nonmoveable jawbones are called the maxillae, the cheekbones are called the zygomas, and the mandible is the lower, moveable portion of the jaw.

The orbit (eye socket) is made up of the frontal bone of the cranium and two facial bones: the maxilla and the zygoma. Together, these bones form a solid bony rim that protrudes around the eye to protect it. The nose mostly consists of flexible cartilage; in fact, only the proximal third of the nose is formed by bone with short bones forming the bridge of the nose.
